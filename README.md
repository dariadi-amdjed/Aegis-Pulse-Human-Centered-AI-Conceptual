# Aegis Pulse

## Human-Centered AI Conceptual Framework for Bystander Trauma Care

---

## 📌 Overview

Aegis Pulse is a student-led conceptual research project developed for the NYAS Junior Academy Challenge.  
It explores how Human-Centered AI, simplified decision flows, and interface design can help bystanders respond more effectively during emergency trauma situations in the critical first minutes after an incident — known as the **“Platinum Ten Minutes.”**

This project does NOT represent a deployed medical system. It is a **conceptual academic framework** combining research, UX design, and theoretical AI systems.

---

## 🧠 Core Idea

The project investigates three main components:

- **Conceptual Visual Assessment** (computer vision-based idea)
- **Binary Triage Logic** (YES / NO decision flow)
- **Local Adaptive Guidance** (simplified emergency instructions)

These concepts are designed to reduce panic and cognitive overload in high-stress environments.

---

## 📊 Research Component

A self-reported survey was conducted with **50 participants (N=50)** across:
- Algeria
- Egypt
- United States

Key findings:
- High lack of first-aid training awareness
- Significant bystander hesitation in emergencies
- Reported delays in emergency response times
